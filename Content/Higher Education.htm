Higher Education

While most people associate Macon with music, it is also a hub for higher education. People from all over the country and even outside the country come to Macon for college. Macon is home to five colleges and universities. Whether you’re looking for a technical degree or a traditional four year undergraduate degree, Macon has got the college or university for you.

 

Central Georgia Technical College

Central Georgia Technical College has been preparing students for the workforce for over 40 years. CGTC offers associate degrees, diplomas, and technical certificates of credit for a range of areas of study.

 

 

 

 

Wesleyan College

Wesleyan College is a women's college focusing on liberal arts. Wesleyan has had its roots in Macon since it was first founded in 1836. Wesleyan has the history and the strength to provide women with a quality education.

 

 

 

 

Mercer University

Mercer University is probably most known to sports fans as the school that knocked duke out of the ring. While Mercer does excel in athletics, it is absolutely exceptional in academics. Mercer is highly ranked in just about every area of study. Engineering, Medicine, Music, and many other colleges within Mercer have been highly sought after.

 

 

 

 

 

Middle Georgia State University

Middle Georgia State University is a public, four year university that offers masters, bachelors, and associates degrees. With five different locations, MGSU can accommodate students in nearly any geographical area of Georgia.

 

 

 

 

Georgia College and State University

Georgia College and State University is one of the best master’s level universities in the south. Located in downtown Macon, it is quite convenient for the working professional.
